Cool-science simulation roadmap

Physical and chemical phenomena made visible with simulation.

This roadmap organizes the portfolio around phenomena: phase changes, dissolution, self-assembly, interfacial behavior, polymer motion, and biomolecular dynamics. Each project should be visually engaging, reproducible, and honest about model limits.

001 — When Hydrogen Bonds Let Go

Complete · water vaporization · TIP4P/2005 · OpenMM · finite NVT slab

Shows how heating broadens a liquid slab and increases the population of water molecules outside the original slab band.

002 — When a Crystal Meets Water

Next build · salt dissolution · NaCl nanocrystal · hydration shells

Visualize ions leaving a small NaCl crystal and becoming hydrated by surrounding water molecules.

003 — Why Oil and Water Separate

Planned · hydrophobic phase separation · polarity · liquid mixtures

Show how polar and nonpolar molecules reorganize into separate regions instead of mixing uniformly.

004 — Molecules That Choose the Interface

Planned · surfactants · amphiphiles · self-organization

Track amphiphilic molecules as they orient at a water/oil or water/vapor interface.

005 — A Polymer Learns to Curl Up

Planned · polymer collapse · solvent quality · soft matter

Use a simple polymer chain to show coil-to-globule behavior and connect to later PLGA nanoparticle work.

006 — Proteins Are Not Statues

Planned · protein breathing · thermal motion · biomolecular dynamics

Show low-amplitude structural fluctuations that make protein structures dynamic rather than rigid.

Selection rules

  • The phenomenon should be visually understandable within a short animation.
  • The model should be defensible enough for public explanation.
  • The page should include a clear “can and cannot claim” note.
  • The workflow should be reproducible without exposing private research data.

Standard project structure

  • Question: what are we trying to see?
  • Model: what force field and engine were used?
  • Visual: what is the public-facing animation?
  • Analysis: what simple metric supports the story?
  • Boundary: what should not be overclaimed?
