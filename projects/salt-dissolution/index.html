Project 002

When a Crystal Meets Water

I wanted to show a familiar chemical event at molecular scale: an ordered salt cluster beginning to erode as water surrounds individual ions.

What to watch

Ions leave the ordered cluster

Gray ions still belong to the crystal-like network. Colored ions have detached from the main cluster and are surrounded by water.

0.00 ns time
0 ions outside the main cluster

The molecular story

Ordered start

The simulation begins from a small crystal-like NaCl cluster surrounded by water.

Surface erosion

Ions near the surface detach first, so the cluster becomes rougher rather than disappearing all at once.

Hydration

Released sodium and chloride ions become wrapped by nearby water molecules.

What happened in this run

In a 1 ns room-temperature simulation, the nanoscale salt cluster did not vanish instantly. Instead, the connected ion network eroded from the surface. By the final frame, 57 ions were outside the largest connected cluster.

How I made it

I placed a small NaCl cluster in TIP4P/2005 water and ran an unrestrained 300 K simulation. For the public page, I focus on the visual sequence: ions leave the connected cluster and become hydrated.

Model note

This is a nanoscale dissolution visualization, not a measurement of real dissolution rate or salt solubility. The useful idea is the molecular picture: ordered ions at the surface detach and water stabilizes them.
