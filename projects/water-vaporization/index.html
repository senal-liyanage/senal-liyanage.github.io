Project 001 · simulation for fun

When Hydrogen Bonds Let Go

A visual molecular-dynamics demonstration of water molecules leaving a heated liquid slab.

water TIP4P/2005 OpenMM vaporization animation

What to watch

Blue molecules stay near the original liquid slab. Orange molecules move into the vapor space as the slab becomes more diffuse.

This is a visual science demo, not a precision boiling-point calculation.

The story in three frames

Liquid band

At the start, most molecules sit in a compact slab.

Interface loosens

Heating increases motion and makes the slab boundary less sharp.

Molecules escape

More waters occupy the vapor region above and below the original slab.

Simulation setup

TIP4P/2005 water was simulated with OpenMM in a finite slab geometry with vapor space along the vertical direction.

4 × 4 × 12 nm³ box NVT 300–600 K

Model note

A nanoscale slab is not the same as a macroscopic pot of water. The point here is to visualize molecular motion and phase-like behavior, not to report vapor pressure or boiling thermodynamics.

Why this belongs in the portfolio

This project sets the style for the portfolio: visual first, chemically honest, and readable by someone who wants to see what molecular simulation can reveal without reading a full research paper.
