Project 001 · simulation for fun

When Hydrogen Bonds Let Go

A molecular-dynamics demonstration of water vaporization from a finite TIP4P/2005 liquid slab. The goal is to make a familiar phenomenon visible at the molecular scale while staying honest about model assumptions.

water TIP4P/2005 OpenMM NVT slab vaporization

What this shows

At low temperature, water remains as a compact liquid slab. As temperature increases, the slab interface broadens and more molecules occupy the vapor region. The browser animation below is a public-facing schematic driven by the simulation summary: blue points represent waters in the original liquid-slab band, and orange points represent waters outside that band.

600 K particle story

Blue points remain inside the original liquid-slab band. Orange points are outside the original band, representing molecules in the vapor/diffuse region.

The dashed lines mark the original liquid-slab boundaries. The counter uses the measured outside-band population from the MD analysis.

The outside-band count increases sharply at high temperature, matching the density-profile result: the 600 K slab no longer behaves like a compact liquid slab and becomes a broad liquid–vapor distribution.

System and method

  • Water model: TIP4P/2005
  • Engine: OpenMM
  • Geometry: liquid slab with vapor space along z
  • Box: 4 × 4 × 12 nm³
  • Ensemble: NVT
  • Temperatures: 300, 373, 450, and 600 K

Model reliability note

This is a qualitative visualization of molecular vaporization from a finite periodic slab. It is not a precision boiling-point, vapor-pressure, or surface-tension calculation. A nanoscale NVT slab does not behave exactly like a macroscopic pot of water at 1 atm.

How to read this project

The important result is not that the 600 K movie is a literal picture of kitchen boiling. The useful idea is simpler and more visual: heating increases molecular motion, the liquid slab becomes less compact, and water molecules increasingly occupy the vapor region.
