Project 001

When Hydrogen Bonds Let Go

I started with something familiar but still molecular: water molecules leaving a heated liquid slab.

What to watch

Blue molecules stay near the original liquid slab. Orange molecules move into the vapor space as the slab becomes more diffuse.

A visual demonstration of molecular motion, not a precision boiling-point calculation.

The molecular story

Liquid band

Most molecules begin in a compact slab.

Interface loosens

Heating increases molecular motion and makes the slab boundary less sharp.

Molecules escape

More waters occupy the vapor region above and below the original slab.

How I made it

I simulated a TIP4P/2005 water slab in OpenMM using a finite box with vapor space along the vertical direction. The animation focuses on the high-temperature case where the vapor region is easiest to see.

Model note

A nanoscale simulation slab is not the same as a macroscopic pot of boiling water. I use this project to show molecular motion and phase-like behavior, not to report a measured boiling point or vapor pressure.
