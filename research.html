Research themes

From simple molecular motion to complex soft-material interfaces.

The portfolio is built around simulation-driven questions that can be visualized clearly, analyzed reproducibly, and explained without exposing private research data.

Current themes

Phase behavior

Molecular dynamics examples that show vaporization, dissolution, and phase separation in a way that can be understood visually.

Polymer nanoparticles

Atomistic models and analysis strategies for PLGA nanoparticle morphology, hydration, solvent organization, and interfacial structure.

Ionic-liquid interfaces

Public-facing summaries of how ion localization, hydration displacement, and orientation metrics can be used to reason about polymer/solvent interfaces.

Computational chemistry workflows

Reusable analysis patterns for molecular simulation, TDDFT post-processing, model preparation, and reproducible scientific computing.

Cool-science boundary

The public roadmap is allowed to be fun and visual, but every project still needs a defensible model choice and a clear statement of what the simulation cannot claim quantitatively.

View roadmap

Portfolio-safe case-study style

Each public simulation should include

  • A clean visual or short animation
  • A concise scientific question
  • A methods summary with software and model assumptions
  • One or two analysis outputs that support the interpretation
  • A clear boundary between public examples and unpublished/private data

Public-content boundary

The public site emphasizes approved visuals, sanitized examples, and reproducible methods. Private project materials stay outside the website.

Research records

Publication and author identifiers are maintained through public scholarly profiles rather than through downloadable private documents on this site.
