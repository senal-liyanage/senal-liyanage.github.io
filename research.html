Research focus

Molecular-level mechanisms in soft materials, nanoparticles, and biomolecular systems.

My research uses molecular simulation, quantum-chemical analysis, and scientific computing to connect atomistic structure, dynamics, interactions, and material behavior.

Current themes

Polymer nanoparticles and drug-delivery materials

Atomistic modeling of PLGA-based nanoparticle systems with attention to morphology, hydration, polymer/solvent organization, and interfacial structure.

Ionic-liquid-modified interfaces

Simulation analysis of ionic-liquid/water environments near polymer interfaces, including ion localization, hydration displacement, and orientation metrics.

Protein-ligand and biomolecular modeling

Docking-oriented and MD-supported workflows for characterizing biomolecular interactions, residue contacts, and structural interpretation.

Quantum-chemical post-processing

TDDFT analysis workflows that connect computed excited-state features and spectral regions to representative molecular structures.

Representative case study: PLGA / ionic-liquid nanoparticle simulations

Scientific question

How does a cholinium carboxylate ionic-liquid/water environment alter the interfacial organization and hydration of PLGA nanoparticles?

Analysis strategy

  • Polymer mass-envelope and radial distribution analysis
  • Water occupancy and hydration-count analysis
  • Ionic-liquid localization near polymer-proximal regions
  • Orientation metrics for ion-pair geometry relative to nanoparticle center of mass
